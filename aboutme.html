About Me

I am a Marketing student at Brigham Young University with a minor in French, currently focused on bridging the gap between creative strategy and technical data analytics. As a sophomore, I’ve dedicated myself to mastering the tools that drive modern business, from SQL and VBA to Tableau and marketing regression models. My perspective is shaped by a deep appreciation for global culture, particularly after spending three months living in Paris, where I fully immersed myself in the language and the French way of life.

In my professional work, I thrive on variety and precision, whether I’m maintaining database integrity as an Alumni Records Specialist or building social media strategies that significantly boost engagement. I enjoy the challenge of taking complex data and turning it into a compelling narrative, a skill I’ve refined through both my coursework and my experience working with local businesses. I am always looking for ways to combine my technical background with a clean, professional aesthetic to create impactful digital experiences.

When I’m not diving into a marketing case study or practicing my French grammar, you can usually find me exploring more tactile and creative hobbies. I love the meditative process of working at a pottery wheel, the energy of swing dancing, and the quiet of a long hike. I’m also a big fan of fantasy literature—often reading French editions to keep my skills sharp—and I value the time I spend with my large family, especially my ten sisters and one brother.
